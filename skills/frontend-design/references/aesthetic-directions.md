# Aesthetic Directions

Use this file when the user has not specified a visual language or when the first draft feels too generic. Pick one direction and execute it consistently rather than blending several into a muddy compromise.

## Editorial / Magazine

- Use assertive headlines, narrow body text, generous white space, and grid tension.
- Let scale contrast carry the design: oversized type, small metadata, sharp rules, and image crops.
- Favor serif or high-character display fonts paired with restrained reading text.

## Brutalist / Raw

- Use hard edges, visible borders, blunt contrast, and intentionally exposed structure.
- Favor bold monospace or grotesk type, blocks of flat color, and abrupt hover states.
- Let the layout feel confrontational but still navigable.

## Luxury / Refined

- Use restraint, dark or muted palettes, precise spacing, and premium material cues.
- Favor elegant serif or fashion-forward display type with quiet body copy.
- Motion should feel smooth and sparse, like a reveal rather than a bounce.

## Retro-Futurist

- Use luminous accents, chrome or glass hints, scan-line or grid textures, and dramatic perspective.
- Pair futuristic display typography with disciplined supporting text.
- Let motion imply instruments, panels, or signal flow rather than generic fades.

## Playful / Toy-Like

- Use rounded forms, exaggerated scale shifts, lively color blocking, and tactile shadows.
- Favor type with personality and high readability.
- Add moments of surprise through bounce, wobble, stacking, or cursor-responsive elements.

## Organic / Natural

- Use warm palettes, irregular curves, soft depth, paper or grain textures, and flowing section transitions.
- Favor humanist or literary typography over sterile UI fonts.
- Let motion feel like drift, growth, or reveal instead of rigid snapping.

## Industrial / Utilitarian

- Use grids, labels, status colors, data density, and durable-looking surfaces.
- Favor condensed, technical, or signage-inspired typography.
- Make controls feel exact and operational rather than decorative.

## Quick Selection Heuristics

- Pick editorial for media, storytelling, portfolios, and fashion-adjacent products.
- Pick brutalist for opinionated tools, creative studios, and projects that should feel raw or anti-corporate.
- Pick luxury for premium services, hospitality, beauty, real estate, or high-ticket commerce.
- Pick retro-futurist for entertainment, music, gaming, sci-fi, or experimental tech products.
- Pick playful for consumer apps, youth products, education, lifestyle, or whimsical brands.
- Pick organic for wellness, food, sustainability, travel, or craft-oriented products.
- Pick industrial for dashboards, ops tooling, hardware, infrastructure, or logistics products.

## Red Flags

- Do not mix three unrelated directions at once.
- Do not reach for generic SaaS gradients and centered cards unless the product truly calls for them.
- Do not let the font choice default to the same safe stack every time.
- Do not add animation everywhere; emphasize a few moments that support the concept.
